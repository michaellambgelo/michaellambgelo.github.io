## The headline talk: "The Potty Mouth Filter"

**Andrew Walling** is coming to present his capstone project, *The Potty Mouth Filter: Building an AI-Powered Profanity Filter for Jellyfin*. It's a .NET plugin that combines OpenAI's Whisper and Vosk speech-recognition models to detect and filter profanity in your media library.

What I love about this talk is the premise. Andrew was a newcomer to AI and speech recognition when he started this project, and he built it because he wanted a cleaner viewing experience on his own self-hosted media server. That's the kind of thing that keeps me excited about running this group -- someone sees a problem in their own living room, picks up a set of tools they've never used before, and ships a real working plugin out the other side. Applied AI for an everyday problem, built by a developer who didn't know the space six months ago.

Andrew is a transitioning Marine and an MSSA student. He found JAWAD through LinkedIn after we put out the [Call for Presentations](/2026/03/jackson-developers-group-announces-call-for-presentations/), reached out directly, and pitched the talk himself. Exactly the pipeline I was hoping to build.

## Call for Presentations: we want you on deck next

This event also has an **open speaker slot** -- 5 to 25 minutes, your topic, your format. Lightning talks, project demos, technical deep dives, career stories, post-mortems on something that blew up in production. Whatever you've got.

I'll be direct: JAWAD is rebuilding post-pandemic, and the single biggest thing we need right now is more local developers willing to stand up in front of twenty peers and share something they know. You don't need to be an expert. You don't need slides. You don't need to have spoken before. This is Andrew's first time speaking to our group.

If you've been on the fence about pitching a talk, this is me asking. Grab the open slot for May 27, or aim at a future month -- either works. Details and the submission form are in the [Call for Presentations post](/2026/03/jackson-developers-group-announces-call-for-presentations/), or you can email me at [jxndevs@michaellamb.dev](mailto:jxndevs@michaellamb.dev).
