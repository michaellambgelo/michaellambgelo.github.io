## Call for Presentations

The Jackson Area Web and App Developers Group is accepting presentation proposals for the remainder of 2026. If you have something you're working on, something you've learned, or something you're passionate about in the world of software development, we want to hear from you.

I've been organizing developer meetups in the Jackson area for a while now, and the thing that keeps these events meaningful is the people who show up and share what they know. A meetup without presentations is just a room full of people staring at each other. Presentations give us something to rally around, something to react to, and something to learn from. They're the heartbeat of a developer community.

## What we're looking for

We're open to a wide range of topics. If it's relevant to software development, web development, app development, DevOps, design, or the tech industry in general, it's fair game. Here are some ideas to get you thinking:

- **Project showcases** -- Show us what you've been building. Side projects, open source contributions, work projects (where appropriate), hackathon results, anything.
- **Technical deep dives** -- Walk us through a technology, framework, language, or tool you've been using. What makes it interesting? What are the gotchas?
- **Lessons learned** -- Share a story about something that went wrong and what you took away from it. These are often the most valuable talks.
- **Career and professional development** -- How you landed your first dev job, how you transitioned into tech, how you manage your time, how you stay motivated.
- **Lightning talks** -- Got something to share but don't need a full session? Five to ten minutes is plenty.

You don't need to be an expert. You don't need to have given a talk before. Some of the best presentations I've seen at meetups came from people who were nervous and unsure and did it anyway. If you're on the fence, consider this your sign.

## Timeline

We're accepting proposals on a rolling basis through the end of 2026. Meetup dates and scheduling will depend on speaker availability and venue logistics, so the sooner you submit, the more flexibility we'll have in finding a time that works for you.

Here's what to expect after you submit:

1. **Submit your proposal** through the form or by email.
2. **I'll follow up** within a week to confirm receipt and discuss scheduling.
3. **We'll coordinate a date** that works for you and the group.
4. **You present** at a Jackson Developers Group meetup.

## What to expect as a presenter

Our meetups are informal and supportive. You'll be presenting to a group of developers and tech enthusiasts in the Jackson area who are genuinely interested in learning from each other. This isn't a conference stage with a thousand people -- it's a room full of peers.

You're welcome to use slides, live code, a whiteboard, or nothing at all. Whatever format works best for your topic. We'll make sure you have what you need for A/V setup. Presentations typically run 20 to 45 minutes with time for questions, but we're flexible.

## Get involved

Even if presenting isn't your thing right now, you can still help. Share this post with someone you think would give a great talk. Sometimes people just need a nudge.

You can stay connected with the Jackson Developers Group through our [Meetup](https://www.meetup.com/jackson-area-web-and-app-developers/) page and [Discord](https://discord.gg/4kSfsXj8Vw) server. If you're in the Jackson area and you're interested in software development, you're welcome here.

I'm looking forward to seeing what our community has to share.
