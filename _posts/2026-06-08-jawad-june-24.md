## Two talks about building for a world that won't sit still

This month's lineup pairs two talks that, on the surface, look unrelated — one about organizational resilience, one about building with Claude. Spend a minute with them and the throughline is obvious: the ground is moving under all of us, and the people who understand how the systems work are the ones who get to shape what comes next.

**Chi Chi Chatterjee**, a Director on C Spire's web apps team, opens with *Preparing for the Next Black Swan*. Borrowing from Taleb, he makes the case that disruption — generative AI, workforce pressure, the next thing none of us can see coming — isn't an event you recover from anymore. It's the baseline. His three capabilities (Sense and Respond, Adaptive Architecture, Sustainable Change) give any team a shared language for it, whether or not you write the code.

**Clea McLemore** of Dare2CareComputers follows with *Use Your Development Skills to Create Valuable Artifacts in Claude*. After building 50-plus artifacts herself, her argument is one I keep coming back to: anyone can ship software now, but the people who understand how the system actually works are the ones who ship something good and resilient.

If you write code, both talks are about your craft in an AI-shaped world. If you work alongside developers — as a manager, a designer, a stakeholder — they're about the vocabulary you need to be a real partner in that work. Bring a friend from either camp.
