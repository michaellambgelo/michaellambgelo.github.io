## A real address, finally

Boxd Card has graduated. The little Chrome extension I built to turn a Letterboxd profile into a shareable image card now has its own front door: **[boxd-card.com](https://boxd-card.com)**.

![Boxd Card moved from the beta domain boxd-card.michaellamb.dev to boxd-card.com, with old links 301-redirecting to the new home](/img/2026-06-19-domain-move.png)

For most of its life Boxd Card lived at `boxd-card.michaellamb.dev` — a perfectly fine beta address tucked under my personal domain. It did the job, but it always read like a placeholder. With the extension stable and a handful of people actually using it, it felt like time to move into the real house.

## A proper landing page

The new home isn't just a redirect — it's an actual landing page that shows what the thing does instead of dumping you straight into a tool.

![The new boxd-card.com landing page, showing the hero with a live card preview](/img/2026-06-19-landing-preview.png)

Paste a Letterboxd URL, hit **Generate**, and it hands you off to the [web app](https://boxd-card.com/app/) with your card already rendering. No account, no sign-in, no watermark.

## No two cards look alike

My favorite detail to finally show off: every card Boxd Card renders gets its own background. It samples two of Letterboxd's brand colors at a random angle and drops them under a near-black wash — loud enough to feel alive, quiet enough to keep the posters out front.

![Three Boxd Card examples — Last Four Watched, Favorites, and Recent Diary — each with a unique randomized gradient background](/img/2026-06-19-gradient-cards.png)

Generate the same profile twice and you'll get two different looks. It's a small thing, but it makes every share feel a little bit one-of-a-kind.

## v0.8.0 is on the Chrome Web Store

The move ships inside the extension too. **v0.8.0** is live on the [Chrome Web Store](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/boxd-card/kcholfdhfcojahebmneeeikelffkokdj) — same one-click install, now pointed at the new home for everything it talks to behind the scenes.

If you already have it installed, the update rolls out automatically. If you don't, this is the easiest way in.

## Go make one

Head to **[boxd-card.com](https://boxd-card.com)**, paste in your profile, and see what your film taste looks like as a card. Or [add it to Chrome](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/boxd-card/kcholfdhfcojahebmneeeikelffkokdj) and generate one straight from any Letterboxd page you're on.
