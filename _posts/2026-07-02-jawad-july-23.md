## Two talks on this month's lineup

**Kurt Showmaker, Ph.D.** opens with *AI in the Research Computing Environment*, a look at how scientific computing infrastructure is evolving as generative AI works its way into academic research. If you've ever wondered what "AI" looks like outside of a product roadmap -- in the messier world of research pipelines and shared compute -- this is that talk.

**Clea McLemore** of Dare2CareComputers follows with *Use Your Development Skills to Create Valuable Artifacts in Claude*. Clea was originally slated to give this talk back in June but couldn't make it, so July 23 is the rescheduled version -- worth the wait. Her argument is one I keep coming back to: anyone can ship software now, but the people who understand how the system actually works are the ones who ship something good and resilient.

## JAWAD is how you get the reps in

Both speaker slots for July 23 are filled, but I want to use this post to make something clear: JAWAD runs a meetup practically every month, and we are *always* looking for the next person to stand up and talk. That's the actual point of this group. Not just AI, not just web dev -- a standing, low-stakes room where developers get to practice explaining what they know to a mixed-technical audience. Managers, designers, students, and career-changers show up alongside engineers, so you're not just performing for people who already speak your dialect of software.

If you've never given a talk before, this is a genuinely easy place to start. Five to twenty-five minutes, any format -- slides, live code, a whiteboard, or nothing at all. Lightning talks, project showcases, lessons learned, career stories. You don't need to be an expert, and you don't need to have spoken before; some of the best JAWAD talks have come from people doing it for the first time.

Details on how to pitch a talk for a future month are in the [Call for Presentations post](/2026/03/jackson-developers-group-announces-call-for-presentations/), or just email me directly at [jxndevs@michaellamb.dev](mailto:jxndevs@michaellamb.dev). If you've been putting off proposing something, consider this your nudge.
